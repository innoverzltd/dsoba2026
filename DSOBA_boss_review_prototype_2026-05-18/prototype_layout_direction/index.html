Designer rationale

What changed from the previous version

The reference is not just a dark homepage. Its useful qualities are: a memorable hero, real campus imagery, portrait credibility, gold call-to-actions, simple mobile actions, and clear content zones for news, events and alumni. These four directions keep those strengths but explore different levels of formality, simplicity, youthfulness, and task clarity.

Direction 1

Modern Professional

Closest to the reference. Dark premium surface, gold hierarchy, strong hero, portrait stack, and structured content bands. Best when the client wants the prototype to feel polished, formal and credible without becoming too traditional.

Audience principle: senior alumni, committee members and donors need immediate trust, so the first screen behaves like an official association front door.
Layout principle: the hero gives emotional context, the portrait stack adds real-person credibility, and the three-column band turns attention into concrete routes: news, events and alumni.
Colour principle: #0e1e2b creates authority and contrast; gold is reserved for hierarchy and CTA so important actions feel ceremonial but still clear.

Desktop rationale

The desktop layout uses a wide cinematic hero because public visitors need to understand DSOBA's stature before choosing an action. Portraits on the right create social proof without interrupting the headline. The lower three-column area mirrors the client's sitemap priorities and makes the homepage useful after the brand impression.

Mobile rationale

The mobile layout compresses the same hierarchy into image, headline and large stacked buttons. This avoids making users read dense navigation on a small screen, while still keeping the key public paths visible: News, Heritage/Events, Alumni Directory and Donate/Join.

Direction 2

Editorial Heritage

Elegant and editorial, with #02385c used as the main brand field across the header and hero area. It uses serif typography, archive-style navigation and magazine-like feature cards. Best for presenting DSOBA as an institution with history, continuity and prestige.

Audience principle: heritage pages are not purely transactional; they need to feel curated, credible and worth reading.
Layout principle: the first viewport behaves like an editorial cover: archive index on the left, institutional statement in the centre, and supporting feature cards on the right. This keeps the page formal without becoming a plain brochure.
Colour principle: #02385c is now the dominant surface colour, not a small accent. Warm ivory is used only as a supporting reading surface, while muted gold is reserved for navigation emphasis and CTA warmth. This keeps the design fresh, official and aligned with the logo.

Desktop rationale

The desktop design is intentionally article-like, but the top half now reads clearly as a #02385c brand canvas. The archive index supports formal content such as President's notes, annual dinner history and membership routes. The large serif headline creates a sense of legacy, while the image and feature cards prevent the page from becoming text-only.

Mobile rationale

On mobile, the header and opening headline sit inside the same #02385c brand field, so the direction remains recognisable even on a narrow screen. The page then moves into campus image and a small number of actions, reducing cognitive load while preserving the premium, archive-led feeling.

Direction 3

Community Vibrant

Now reworked with the Get Hyped reference language: oversized headline, pill navigation, tilted cards, bold stats and energetic motion. The content remains DSOBA-focused: Annual Dinner, chapter events, alumni stories, directory and portal access.

Audience principle: younger alumni are more likely to respond to people, events and visible community momentum than formal association copy alone.
Layout principle: the tilted stats/image cards make events and people feel active. The large statement section below the hero keeps the vertical rhythm from the reference while translating it into alumni community messaging.
Effect principle: the reference has many effects, but the prototype should use only the useful ones: floating cards, energetic card angles, pill navigation and bold section reveals. This gives movement without making the association site feel like a campaign microsite only.

Desktop rationale

The desktop frame borrows the reference's memorable composition: a huge black headline, central pill navigation, bright tilted cards and image-led proof. For DSOBA, those cards become Annual Dinner, chapter events and alumni story entry points, so the visual energy still supports real site content.

Mobile rationale

Mobile keeps the same energy in a tighter stack: headline first, then tilted event/stat cards, then direct actions. This avoids copying every desktop effect while preserving the reference's rhythm and making event discovery easy on a phone.

Direction 4

Clean Easy

Practical and service-led, now closer to the Stacker-style hero treatment: a soft animated gradient background, central headline, rounded portal preview, then vertical service modules for alumni actions.

Audience principle: users entering the portal or service flows need orientation more than atmosphere, but a modern animated background helps the page feel current and approachable rather than purely administrative.
Layout principle: the page follows a vertical rhythm: central headline, portal preview, core service modules, then supporting content. This makes the prototype easier to explain because each scroll section maps to a Phase 1 flow.
Colour principle: pastel blue, pink and orange gradients create a light product-site feeling; black gives structure and readability; soft lime highlights primary alumni actions. The animation should feel ambient and slow, supporting the page without distracting from tasks.

Desktop rationale

The desktop frame behaves like a product page for the DSOBA portal. The central headline mirrors modern SaaS landing pages, while the rounded preview makes the portal tangible with real alumni tasks: membership status, Annual Dinner payment and directory visibility. The animated gradient gives energy without changing the alumni-focused content.

Mobile rationale

The mobile frame keeps the same vertical order: headline, primary portal action, then service modules. The gradient stays in the opening section only, so stakeholders get the modern feel without sacrificing readability in the flow-heavy content below.
